Introduction

Building a carbon capture unit and an air quality sensor for quantification

Amidst the public conversation we decided that more focus should be put on the quality of the air we breathe, and we aimed on proposing our own solution. For this project, we have constructed an Air Quality Monitor (AQM) from scratch through connecting sensors to an arduino microcontroller. A Carbon Capture Unit (CCU) was also constructed, using household items, to capture carbon from the surrounding air by utilising a simple chemical reaction. The primary aim of this project was to test the effectiveness of the AQM in measuring air quality and the effectiveness of the CCU in removing carbon from said air. The AQM can be calibrated by comparing its readings with pre-existing publicly accessible monitors. Then we can compare the air quality readings before and after we introduce our CCU, and in doing so testing the effectiveness of the unit. Further aims of this project include comparing the effectiveness of our CCU to other means of carbon capture, such as plants
